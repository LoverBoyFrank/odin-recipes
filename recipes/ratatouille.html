Ratatouille

Description

Ratatouille is a classic French vegetable dish that celebrates fresh, simple ingredients. Tender slices of zucchini, eggplant, and squash are layered or gently simmered with tomatoes, onion, and garlic, creating a dish that is both colorful and comforting with rich, natural flavors.

Slow-cooked until the vegetables are soft and fragrant, ratatouille is finished with olive oil and herbs that bring everything together. Light yet satisfying, it can be served on its own, over rice or pasta, or as a flavorful side dish.

Ingredients

  • Zucchini
  • Eggplant
  • Tomato
  • Bell pepper
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Fresh herbs (thyme or basil)

Steps

  1. Prep veggies: Thinly slice eggplant, zucchini, yellow squash, and tomatoes.
  2. Make sauce: Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil, add crushed tomatoes, salt, pepper, and herbs. Spread in a baking dish.
  3. Arrange: Layer sliced vegetables upright in the dish, alternating types.
  4. Season: Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle salt, pepper, and herbs.
  5. Cover & bake: Cover with foil and bake at 375°F / 190°C for 40 minutes.
  6. Uncover & finish: Remove foil and bake 15 to 20 more minutes until tender.
  7. Serve: Rest a few minutes, garnish with fresh basil or thyme.
